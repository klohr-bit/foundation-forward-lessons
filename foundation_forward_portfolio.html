Kimberly Lohr

Learning Experience Designer

Good learning design starts with one question: what does this person actually need to be able to do? Not what content needs to be covered. Not what format looks impressive. What does the learner need, and what is the most direct path to get them there?

That question leads to experiences that are more focused than people expect and more effective than they thought possible. It also leads to systems — ones that scale, serve more than one purpose at once, and keep working long after the build is done.

The work that matters most to me is the work done with people who care deeply about what they are building. The coach who has spent twenty years refining her methodology. The childcare director obsessed with getting it right for every kid in her center. The photographer who sees what other people miss and wants to teach others to see it too.

That passion is the raw material. My job is to build the learning experience around it.

Johns Hopkins University — Center for Technology in Education

From slide decks to a six-state training system that runs without us in the room.

The need

Train educators on a complex assessment system across multiple states. Every new cohort, every new hire required the team to show up. No way to scale, no way to keep pace.

What we built

A core training system with unique learning paths for six states, wrapped in a train the trainer model. Asynchronous options added as states asked for more flexibility. The system flexed without losing its integrity.

What changed

More educators reached. Trainers onboard new staff independently. Satisfaction improved with on-demand access. Team presence no longer required.

02

The CoLLab Field Hockey

A content system that grows an audience, builds a tool library, and scopes a future course — all at once.

Active

The need

400 Substack subscribers, deep expertise, no system behind it. Needed content that built the audience consistently and a pathway toward a paid product when the time was right.

What we built

A 32-week content system across four coaching pillars. Every post serves two audiences at once — coaches reading the Substack and players receiving interactive drills before practice. A branded tool library grows with every post.

What we are building toward

A paid course built on evidence, not guesswork. The content system generates audience data across every post so when she is ready to launch, she knows exactly what her audience will pay for.

03

Iris B Photography

Turning photography expertise into interactive experiences that teach animal behavior before the shot.

First Tool Live

The need

Wildlife photography instruction goes beyond technique — it requires reading animal behavior in the field. That skill cannot be taught from a static post. The learning has to feel like being there.

What we are building

Mini learning experiences built around real field scenarios. Learners read what an animal is doing, make a decision, and receive feedback connecting behavior to the photographic moment. Short enough for a phone. Specific enough to matter.

What we are building toward

A growing library of behavior-based experiences that can be bundled into a paid course, used as a lead generation tool, or offered as a premium workshop resource.
