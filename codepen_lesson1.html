You Already Have a Course
Most small business owners are sitting on one right now. They just don't know it yet.

Think about the last time you explained your process to a new client. Or wrote an email breaking something down because it was easier than answering the same question again. Or had that conversation — the one you've had ten times — where you walk someone through exactly how something works.

That's all content. And it's yours.

So why isn't it making you money while you sleep? Because dumping information on someone is not the same as teaching them. Content tells people what you know. A course changes what they can do. And people pay for the change, not the information.

The difference between a social post and a course isn't production quality or platform. It's whether there's a real outcome waiting at the end.

By the time you finish these five lessons you'll know exactly what makes a course worth paying for — and why most business owners get it wrong before they ever hit record.

What you'll walk away with

A clear understanding of the difference between sharing content and designing a learning experience — and why that difference is the most important thing to get right before you build anything.
