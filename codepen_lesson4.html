Your Turn — Name Your Outcome
This is the most important two minutes in this entire module. Don't skip it.

Most people start building a course by thinking about topics. What do I know? What could I cover? How many modules should I make?

⚠
That is the wrong starting point. Every time. Organizing topics produces content. It does not produce a course. The difference is one question.

What do I want my student to be able to do when they finish? Not understand. Not appreciate. Do differently than they could before.

Everything else — the structure, the lessons, the exercises — gets built around that answer. Without it you are just organizing information. With it you are designing a result.

Think about something you already teach, explain, or do repeatedly for clients.
Write one sentence. What will someone be able to do after learning from you that they could not do before? Not understand — do.
Save this. Screenshot it, drop it in your notes app, write it on a sticky note. This sentence is the foundation of your course — the moment you named an outcome instead of a topic.

If that was hard to answer, you are not behind. That is exactly where most people are when they start. Naming the outcome is the work. It is also why people hire someone to help them build this instead of trying to figure it out alone.
